Indian Election System

The Indian election system is one of the largest and most complex democratic exercises in the world.

✔ 900+ million voters

✔ Multi-phase elections

✔ Advanced EVM technology

What is an Election?

What is Election?

An election is a process where people vote to choose their representatives in government.

In India

India is the world's largest democracy. Every citizen above 18 years has the right to vote.

History of Indian Elections

After independence, India held its first general election in 1951-52. It was a massive exercise that proved democracy could work in a diverse and large country like India. The Election Commission of India was established in 1950 to conduct free and fair elections.

Important Timeline

1950

Election Commission Formed

25 January 1950 – Election Commission of India established.

1952

First General Election

India's first Lok Sabha election held successfully.

1989

Voting Age Reduced

From 21 to 18 years.

2004

EVMs Used Nationwide

Electronic Voting Machines introduced across India.

Role of Election Commission

The Election Commission ensures free and fair elections in India.

  • ✔ Maintains voter list
  • ✔ Conducts elections
  • ✔ Enforces Model Code of Conduct
